Methodology

Budget Categories

Federal spending is organized into 10 major categories for clarity and educational purposes. These align with OMB budget functions but are simplified for public understanding:

  1. Social Security - OASDI + SSI
  2. Medicare - Federal health insurance for seniors
  3. National Defense - Department of Defense
  4. Interest on National Debt - Net interest payments
  5. Healthcare (Non-Medicare) - Medicaid, CHIP, ACA
  6. Veterans Benefits - VA healthcare, disability, education
  7. Income Security - SNAP, housing, unemployment, tax credits
  8. Education - Federal student aid, K-12 support
  9. Transportation - Highways, transit, aviation, rail
  10. Everything Else - All other federal programs

Subcategory Allocation

Subcategory dollar amounts are based on:

  • Official agency budget documents where available
  • Pro-rata allocation based on historical spending patterns
  • CBO and OMB projections for FY 2024
  • Actual outlays reported in FY 2024 year-end financial reports

Fiscal Year 2024

The U.S. federal fiscal year runs from October 1, 2023 to September 30, 2024. This data reflects actual outlays (spending) rather than budget authority or appropriations.

Data Verification

All data can be independently verified through official government sources. Complete citations with direct links are available in the CITATIONS_2024.md file in our GitHub repository.

Accuracy & Limitations

  • All percentages rounded to two decimal places and adjusted to sum to exactly 100%
  • Some subcategory breakdowns are estimates based on historical patterns where exact data is unavailable
  • Mandatory vs. discretionary spending not separated in this educational tool
  • Off-budget spending (e.g., Social Security) included in totals
  • Emergency and supplemental appropriations included where enacted
